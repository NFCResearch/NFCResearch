Unlocking the Secrets of ID Cards: Exploring Wireless Magic of Near Field Communication with Software-Defined Radios

This research demo provides a comprehensive, hands-on introduction to Near Field Communication (NFC) technology and Software Defined Radio (SDR) for signal capture and analysis. They are designed to equip students with practical skills in wireless communication. The demo guides users through the setup of hardware and software tools, including an Arduino Uno, PN5180 NFC reader, and SDR# software for capturing and processing NFC signals. Students explore NFC communication protocols at the 13.56 MHz frequency, gaining a deep understanding of both reader-to-card and card-to-reader interactions. The demo details the wiring and configuration of NFC systems and the use of Python for signal analysis. Through step-by-step instructions, students learn to capture, visualize, and interpret NFC signals, applying signal decoding and protocol analysis techniques. With a focus on practical application, the demo emphasizes using SDR to capture real-time NFC communications, enhancing students' understanding of analog and digital signal processing. This demo serves as a critical learning tool for those interested in wireless technologies, providing insights into the design, capture, and analysis of NFC systems with broader applications in mobile payments, access control, and other contactless systems.
